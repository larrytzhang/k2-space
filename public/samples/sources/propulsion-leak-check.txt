PROCEDURE: PROPULSION FEED SYSTEM PRESSURE DECAY LEAK CHECK
Document No: PROP-INSP-1108
Revision: B
Effective Date: 2026-01-30

PURPOSE
Verify that the hydrazine feed system is within allowable leakage limits prior to
propellant loading.

SCOPE
Applies to the monopropellant feed manifold P/N 30918-FLT between the tank isolation
valve and the thruster manifold cluster.

ROLES
Test Conductor (TC)
Propulsion Engineer (PE)
Safety Officer (SO)
Quality Assurance (QA)

EQUIPMENT
Helium supply bottle, grade 5.0, regulated (Qty: 1)
Digital pressure transducer, 0–3000 psig, ± 0.1% FS (Qty: 1)
Soap solution, propellant-compatible (Qty: 1 bottle)
Calibrated stopwatch (Qty: 1)
Thermocouple, Type T, surface mount (Qty: 1)

1. SETUP

  WARNING: Hydrazine residue may be present. Personnel shall wear Level B PPE including
  SCBA until chemical clearance is signed off by the Safety Officer.

  1.1 Confirm system is isolated and vented to atmosphere.
      System pressure: _____ psig (acceptance: < 1 psig)
      Pass / Fail: _____
      PE signature: _____________

  1.2 Install calibrated pressure transducer at test port TP-3.
      Transducer S/N: _____________
      Calibration due date: _____________

  1.3 CAUTION: Do not exceed regulator outlet of 350 psig. Overpressure will damage
      the downstream regulator diaphragm.

2. PRESSURIZATION

  2.1 Slowly open helium supply valve. Increase pressure at < 50 psi/min.

  2.2 Stabilize system at 300 ± 5 psig.
      Observed pressure: _____ psig
      System temperature: _____ °C

  2.3 Close isolation valve ISO-2. System is now sealed.

  2.4 NOTE: Allow 10 minutes for thermal equilibrium before beginning decay window.

3. DECAY MEASUREMENT

  3.1 Record initial conditions at t = 0.
      P_initial: _____ psig
      T_initial: _____ °C
      Timestamp: _____________

  3.2 Wait 30 minutes with system isolated.

  3.3 Record final conditions at t = 30 min.
      P_final: _____ psig
      T_final: _____ °C

  3.4 Compute temperature-corrected pressure decay.
      ΔP corrected: _____ psi
      Acceptance: ΔP ≤ 0.5 psi over 30 minutes
      Pass / Fail: _____

4. EXTERNAL LEAK SURVEY

  4.1 While system is pressurized, apply soap solution to all mechanical joints.

  4.2 WARNING: Do not apply soap solution to electrical connectors or heater leads.
      Ingress may cause short circuits upon reenergization.

  4.3 Inspect each joint for 15 seconds minimum. Record any bubbling.
      Joint J-1 observation: ___ No leak ___ Leak observed
      Joint J-2 observation: ___ No leak ___ Leak observed
      Joint J-3 observation: ___ No leak ___ Leak observed
      Joint J-4 observation: ___ No leak ___ Leak observed

5. SHUTDOWN

  5.1 Vent system through vent valve VV-1 at < 50 psi/min.

  5.2 Confirm system pressure returned to atmospheric.
      Final pressure: _____ psig

  5.3 Remove test equipment and cap test port TP-3.

  5.4 Sign-offs:
      TC: _____________
      PE: _____________
      QA: _____________
      SO: _____________

ESTIMATED DURATION: 75 minutes
