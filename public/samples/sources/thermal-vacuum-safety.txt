PROCEDURE: THERMAL VACUUM CHAMBER ENTRY AND SAFETY LOCKOUT
Document No: SAFE-TVAC-0042
Revision: D
Effective Date: 2026-03-03

PURPOSE
Establish the safety interlock, lockout, and personnel entry sequence for the 6-meter
thermal vacuum chamber TVAC-A before any personnel access the chamber interior.

SCOPE
Applies to all personnel accessing the interior of TVAC-A at any time, including during
test, maintenance, and emergency response.

ROLES
Safety Officer (SO)
Chamber Operator (CO)
Test Conductor (TC)
Authorized Entrant (AE)

EQUIPMENT
Oxygen meter, portable, calibrated (Qty: 2)
Lockout/Tagout kit (Qty: 1)
Intrinsically safe flashlight (Qty: 1 per entrant)
Two-way radio (Qty: 1 per entrant)
Harness and lanyard, fall arrest rated (Qty: 1 per entrant)

1. CHAMBER DEPRESSURIZATION VERIFICATION

  WARNING: Attempting to open the chamber door against residual vacuum can cause the
  door to swing violently, resulting in severe crushing injury or death. Never bypass
  the pressure equalization interlock.

  1.1 Confirm chamber pressure equalization valve is OPEN.

  1.2 Verify chamber pressure reading.
      Chamber pressure: _____ Torr (required: ≥ 750 Torr before door unlock)
      Pass / Fail: _____
      CO signature: _____________

  1.3 WARNING: If pressure reading disagrees with ambient by more than 5 Torr,
      STOP. Do not attempt entry. Contact SO immediately.

2. THERMAL SAFING

  2.1 Verify all shroud zones are at SAFE temperature (between 10 °C and 35 °C).
      Zone A: _____ °C
      Zone B: _____ °C
      Zone C: _____ °C
      Zone D: _____ °C
      Pass / Fail: _____

  2.2 CAUTION: Cold surfaces may condense atmospheric moisture when the chamber is
      opened, creating slip hazards and potential damage to flight hardware.

  2.3 If any zone is outside SAFE range, initiate warm-up and wait until criteria met.

3. ELECTRICAL LOCKOUT

  WARNING: High-voltage feed-throughs remain energized until verified disconnected.
  Contact with live feed-throughs may result in fatal electrical shock.

  3.1 De-energize all chamber electrical panels at the master disconnect.

  3.2 Apply personal locks to each panel. Each authorized entrant applies their own lock.

    3.2.1 Panel PNL-1 locked by: _____________
    3.2.2 Panel PNL-2 locked by: _____________
    3.2.3 Panel PNL-3 locked by: _____________

  3.3 Attempt to energize panels via control interface. Confirm NO activation.
      Control attempt result: ___ Blocked ___ Activated

  3.4 NOTE: If control attempt shows activation, lockout has failed. Do not enter.
      Escalate to SO.

4. ATMOSPHERIC VERIFICATION

  4.1 Insert oxygen meter through inspection port.

  4.2 Confirm oxygen concentration is within safe range.
      O2 reading: _____ % (acceptance: 19.5–23.5%)
      Pass / Fail: _____
      SO signature: _____________

  4.3 WARNING: Oxygen concentration outside the acceptance range indicates residual
      inert gas. Do NOT enter chamber. Ventilate and re-test.

5. ENTRY AUTHORIZATION

  5.1 Confirm radio communication check with external monitor.
      Radio check: ___ Good ___ Poor

  5.2 Don fall-arrest harness. Verify anchor point rating of 5000 lbf minimum.

  5.3 SO grants entry authorization.
      SO authorization signature: _____________
      Time of entry: _____________

  5.4 Authorized entrant(s) enter chamber.
      AE 1 name: _____________
      AE 2 name: _____________

6. EXIT AND RESTORATION

  6.1 All personnel exit chamber and confirm headcount.

  6.2 Remove all personal locks.

  6.3 Restore chamber control authority to CO.
      TC signature: _____________
      CO signature: _____________
      SO signature: _____________
      Time closeout: _____________

ESTIMATED DURATION: 45 minutes
